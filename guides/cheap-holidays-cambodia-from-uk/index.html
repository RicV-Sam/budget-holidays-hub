Cambodia has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. If you're exploring Southeast Asia, budget travel in Vietnam or cheap holidays to Thailand are excellent additions to your itinerary. Whether you are planning to explore the awe-inspiring Angkor Wat, visit the bustling Royal Palace in Phnom Penh, or discover the serene riverside of Kampot, Cambodia offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Cambodia provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on ancient Khmer history and incredible street food, you can uncover the true essence of Cambodia while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Cambodia

The cheapest months to visit Cambodia from the UK are:

  • May to September

The wet season brings lush green landscapes and the lowest prices for accommodation, often with 50% discounts on luxury hotels.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick

You'll usually fly into Phnom Penh or Siem Reap with a stopover in Bangkok or Singapore.

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Rent a bicycle to explore Siem Reap and the outskirts
  • Stay in 'Boutique Hostels' which offer hotel-quality rooms at hostel prices
  • Eat at local markets for the best value food
  • Buy a multi-day pass for Angkor Wat to get the best value for your visit

Best Areas to Stay in Cambodia for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Cambodia. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as Siem Reap for temple access, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Cambodia, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the lively capital of Phnom Penh or the relaxed riverside in Kampot, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Cambodia that many tourists miss.
