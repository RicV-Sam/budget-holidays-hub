Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat Like a Local at Food Courts: For clean, incredibly cheap, and varied food, head to the food courts in malls like Terminal 21 in Bangkok. Prices are often even lower than street stalls.
  • Download the Bolt App: Avoid the "tourist price" for Tuk-Tuks and taxis. Bolt (and Grab) offers transparent, fixed pricing for cars and motorbikes across major Thai cities.
  • Travel by Sleeper Train: Save on a night's accommodation and move between cities by booking a 2nd class sleeper train from Bangkok to Chiang Mai or the southern islands.
  • Utilize 7-Eleven: Thailand's 7-Elevens are legendary for a reason. They offer the cheapest bottled water, surprisingly good hot snacks, and essential supplies at local prices.

Best Areas to Stay in Thailand for Budget Travellers

To maximize your budget, consider these value-packed neighbourhoods:

  • Bangkok (Banglamphu/Samsen): Just a short walk from the chaos of Khao San Road, the Samsen area offers quiet, affordable guesthouses and a more local atmosphere.
  • Chiang Mai (Old City): Staying within the historic walls gives you walking access to hundreds of temples, markets, and the city's best budget-friendly eateries.
  • Koh Lanta (South): A much more relaxed and affordable island alternative to Phuket or Koh Samui, with a wide range of budget beachfront bungalows.
