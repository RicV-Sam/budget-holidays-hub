For many UK holidaymakers, the Canary Islands represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. For specific details on each island, see our guides to cheap holidays to Tenerife and budget travel in Lanzarote . From the moment you arrive to see the volcanic peaks of Mount Teide or wander around the sand dunes of Maspalomas, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to the Canary Islands is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets the Canary Islands apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the lunar landscapes of Lanzarote or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing year-round sunshine and diverse volcanic terrains and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Canary Islands

The cheapest months to visit the Canary Islands from the UK are:

  • January and February (excluding half-term)
  • May and June
  • November

Avoiding school holidays is the key to finding the lowest prices for these popular islands.

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Duty-free status means alcohol, tobacco, and perfume are very cheap
  • Rent a car to explore the interior—it's often cheaper than organized tours
  • Eat at 'Guachinches' in Tenerife for authentic and cheap local food
  • Use the reliable 'TITSA' bus network in Tenerife or 'Global' in Gran Canaria

Best Areas to Stay in the Canary Islands for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in the Canary Islands, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like Los Cristianos in Tenerife, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as Las Palmas in Gran Canaria and Puerto del Carmen in Lanzarote are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of the Canary Islands allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.
