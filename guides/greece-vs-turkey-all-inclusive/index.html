Greece vs Turkey All-Inclusive 2026: Cheapest, Best Resorts and Winner

Quick verdict: Choose Turkey if you want the cheapest all-inclusive package, bigger resort facilities and the most value from Antalya or Dalaman. Choose Greece if island atmosphere, tavernas and easy exploring matter more than the lowest resort price.

Last updated: March 2026

Quick Answer: Turkey Wins on Price, Greece Wins on Atmosphere

Decision Point Winner Why it matters
Cheapest all-inclusive holiday Turkey Antalya and Dalaman often price lower than Crete or Rhodes for comparable all-inclusive resorts, especially at 4-star and 5-star level.
Best 5-star resort value Turkey Large resort complexes usually include more pools, dining and family facilities for the same budget.
Best local atmosphere Greece Greek islands are stronger if you want villages, tavernas and independent exploring.
Best for families Turkey Bigger hotels often mean more slides, kids' clubs, snack bars and entertainment included.

The 2026 Verdict: Turkey vs Greece

  • Winner on price: Turkey, because Antalya and Dalaman packages are often around 25-35% cheaper than similar Greek island options.
  • Winner on atmosphere: Greece, because Crete, Rhodes and smaller islands are easier for tavernas, harbour walks and local exploring.
  • Winner on resort facilities: Turkey, especially for large family resorts, waterparks and ultra all-inclusive hotels.

Key Takeaways

  • Choose Turkey if your priority is the lowest all-inclusive price or the biggest hotel facilities for your budget.
  • Choose Greece if you want more independent exploring, island character and meals outside the hotel.
  • For most UK families comparing package holidays, Turkey is the stronger value pick in 2026.

Which Is Cheaper: Greece or Turkey All-Inclusive?

Turkey is usually cheaper than Greece for all-inclusive holidays from the UK, especially if you are comparing resort-based packages in Antalya, Dalaman, Crete and Rhodes. Turkey tends to win on headline price, bigger hotel facilities and 5-star value, while Greece can be worth paying more for if you want island towns, independent meals and easier exploring outside the hotel.

For both destinations, May, June and September are usually the lowest-price windows for UK packages while still offering warm weather.

Start with London Gatwick, Manchester and Birmingham for the widest spread of direct package and low-cost options to both Turkey and Greece.

Resort Quality: Turkey Usually Gives More for the Money

Turkey specializes in massive, purpose-built all-inclusive complexes. In regions like Lara Beach or Belek, the infrastructure is designed for high-volume luxury. You will often find multiple pools, private waterparks, and 10+ dining options within a single resort. Greece tends to have smaller, more intimate hotels. While Greek 5-star resorts are stunning, they often carry a "Eurozone premium" that makes them 30-40% more expensive than comparable Turkish options.

Food and Dining: Turkey for Variety, Greece for Local Atmosphere

If you prefer variety within your hotel, Turkey is the champion. The "Ultra All-Inclusive" concept is standard in many Antalya resorts, often including 24-hour food and international brands. However, if you enjoy leaving the resort to eat, Greece wins. The quality of local Greek ingredients and the charm of a seaside taverna is hard to match, though these meals will be an additional cost on top of your package.

Why Turkey Is Often Cheaper for UK Travellers

In 2026, the strength of the Pound against the Turkish Lira continues to make Turkey a haven for bargain hunters. Even within an all-inclusive stay, any extras you buy (spa treatments, excursions, local shopping) will be significantly cheaper in Turkey than in the Euro-based Greek islands.

  • Choose Turkey if: You want maximum luxury for the minimum price and plan to spend most of your time enjoying resort facilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for families, Greece or Turkey?

Turkey often wins for families due to the sheer scale of the resorts, many of which feature on-site waterparks and 24/7 snacks included in the price.

Is the food better in Greek or Turkish hotels?

Turkish resorts offer more variety and quantity (buffets are legendary). Greek hotels often focus more on quality and fresh, local Mediterranean ingredients.

Do I need a visa for Turkey from the UK?

No, as of 2026, UK citizens do not require a visa for tourist visits to Turkey for up to 90 days. This makes it just as easy as visiting Greece.

Is the water safe to drink?

In both destinations, it is recommended to stick to bottled or filtered water, which is almost always included for free in your all-inclusive package.
