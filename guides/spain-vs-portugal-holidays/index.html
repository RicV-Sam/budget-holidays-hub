Spain vs Portugal holidays

Last updated: April 2026

💡 Quick Answer

Winner overall: Spain. Spain wins for cheaper entry-level package prices, more flight routes from UK airports, and wider resort choice. Portugal wins if you care more about relaxed atmosphere, coastal scenery, and quality local food over absolute lowest cost.

Side-by-side comparison table

Category Spain Portugal
Cost 7-night package: approx. £320-£560 7-night package: approx. £360-£620
Flights from UK Very high volume from London, Manchester, Birmingham, Bristol, Leeds Strong coverage, especially Faro/Lisbon/Porto, but fewer total routes
Accommodation Huge range from budget aparthotels to all-inclusive mega resorts Smaller-scale hotels, apartments, and boutique coastal stays
Food and drink Great low-cost tapas and menu del dia options Excellent seafood value and generally strong restaurant quality
Best for Families, first-time package travellers, nightlife variety Couples, scenic road trips, calmer beach towns

Cost comparison

Spain is usually cheaper on base package price because competition is intense across the Costas, Balearics, and Canaries. Portugal can match Spain during shoulder season, but peak dates and premium Algarve resorts often narrow the value gap. If total spend is your priority, start with our Spain main guide and then compare against cheap holidays to Portugal from the UK.

Best destinations

  • Top Spain picks: Costa Blanca for low prices, Costa del Sol for family infrastructure, Mallorca for mixed beach and town breaks.
  • Top Portugal picks: Algarve (Albufeira/Lagos) for beach stays, Lisbon for city-break plus coast, Porto for culture-led weekends.

If you are choosing Spain, compare timing and niche value with Spain in June, Spain in September, family holidays in Spain, and our cheapest places in Spain breakdown.

When to go

For both countries, May, June, and September usually deliver the best value-weather balance. July and August bring the highest prices and busiest resorts. Spain tends to have slightly stronger late-season inventory from UK tour operators, while Portugal can feel less crowded in shoulder months.

Pros and cons

Spain: pros and cons

  • Pros: Lower average package prices, huge airport choice, broad resort styles.
  • Pros: Very strong for families and all-inclusive planning.
  • Cons: Some hotspots feel crowded in peak season.
  • Cons: Quality varies more between resort zones.

Portugal: pros and cons

  • Pros: Excellent food quality and scenic coastline.
  • Pros: Relaxed pace in many towns, good for couples.
  • Cons: Fewer ultra-cheap package options than Spain.
  • Cons: Top Algarve locations can get expensive in school holidays.

Which should you choose?

Choose Spain if you want the best odds of finding a low-cost package from your nearest UK airport, especially for families or mixed-age groups. Choose Portugal if you are happy to pay a little more for a calmer pace, stronger food scene, and a coastal town vibe that feels less resort-heavy.

Frequently asked questions

Is Spain or Portugal cheaper for a package holiday from the UK?

Spain is usually slightly cheaper overall because there are more flights and more budget package options across multiple regions.

Which is better for families: Spain or Portugal?

Spain normally wins for families due to wider all-inclusive choice, shorter transfer options, and larger family-focused resort infrastructure.

Which destination has better food value?

Portugal often edges it on restaurant quality-to-price, especially seafood. Spain still offers excellent value in tapas bars and lunch menus.

When is the cheapest time to visit Spain or Portugal?

May, early June, and September are commonly the best-value periods for weather and price.
