Corfu has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the Venetian-style Corfu Old Town, visit the stunning Paleokastritsa bay, or discover the historic Achilleion Palace, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Corfu provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. If you enjoy Greek islands, you might also like our budget guide to Santorini . By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on lush green landscapes and crystal-clear Ionian waters, you can uncover the true essence of Corfu while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Corfu

The cheapest months to visit Corfu from the UK are:

  • May and June
  • September and October

The shoulder season offers warm weather and clear skies, but without the peak-summer crowds or prices.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • East Midlands
  • Bristol

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat 'Gyros' for a delicious, filling, and very cheap meal
  • Use the 'Green Buses' for affordable travel across the whole island
  • Stay in smaller villages slightly inland for much lower room rates
  • Take advantage of free beaches—most are easily accessible

Best Areas to Stay in Corfu for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Corfu. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the charming Corfu Town, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Corfu, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring budget resorts in Sidari or the social hub of Kavos, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Corfu that many tourists miss.
