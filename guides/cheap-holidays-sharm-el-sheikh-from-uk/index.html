Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Go All-Inclusive: Sharm is one of the few places where all-inclusive actually saves money. It covers your food, drinks, and snacks, allowing you to leave your wallet in the safe for most of the trip.
  • Shop at the Old Market: For souvenirs, spices, and authentic local food, skip the malls and head to the "Old Market" (Sharm al-Maya). It's much cheaper and offers a more traditional Egyptian experience.
  • Shore Snorkel for Free: Many hotels have their own house reefs. You don't need an expensive boat trip to see incredible marine life; just grab a mask and swim out from your resort jetty.
  • Book Excursions Locally: Avoid booking tours through your UK holiday rep. Reputable local shops in Naama Bay or the Old Market offer the same desert safaris and boat trips at much lower prices.

Best Areas to Stay in Sharm El Sheikh for Budget Travellers

To find the best value and avoid the highest prices of the ultra-luxury zones, consider these areas:

  • Naama Bay (Off-Beach): The city's main hub. Staying in a hotel just a 10-minute walk back from the beach can save you up to 40% while keeping you close to the action.
  • Old Town (Hadaba): Located on a cliff top, this area offers some of the most affordable guesthouses and older resorts with spectacular views and great access to the local market.
  • Shark's Bay: A smaller, more relaxed bay that often features excellent value boutique hotels and some of the best shore-snorkeling spots in Sharm.
